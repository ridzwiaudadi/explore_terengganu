Introduction



Terengganu, located on the eastern coast of Peninsular Malaysia, is a state known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. It boasts pristine beaches with crystal-clear waters, such as those on Redang Island and Perhentian Islands, making it a paradise for beach lovers and divers. The state's landscape also features lush rainforests and charming fishing villages, providing a glimpse into a simpler, traditional way of life.




Terengganu is also famous for its vibrant culture, which includes traditional arts like batik-making, songket weaving, and wayang kulit (shadow puppetry). The capital city, Kuala Terengganu, is home to landmarks such as the Crystal Mosque and the Terengganu State Museum, which is the largest museum in Southeast Asia. Visitors can also explore the local markets to taste delicious traditional foods like keropok lekor and nasi dagang, adding to the state's appeal as a destination that blends nature, culture, and history seamlessly.
